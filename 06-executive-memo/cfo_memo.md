# CFO Memo - Q3 2026 Board Update

**To:** CFO and Board of Directors  
**From:** FP&A  
**Subject:** Q3 performance, FY2026 outlook, and recommended actions

Q3 revenue finished **$437K below budget**, with the miss concentrated in July and August as new-logo volume and close timing weakened. Price/mix remained supportive, and September execution improved, indicating a conversion problem rather than broad pricing pressure. FY opex is tracking **$871K unfavorable**, led by Marketing ($550K) and Sales ($454K), partly offset by Customer Success and G&A savings.

The Base case forecasts **$52.5M FY revenue**, **$58.3M ending ARR**, and **$6.7M ending cash**. The model indicates approximately **12.3 months of ending runway** under the selected assumptions. Upside revenue is $55.9M; downside is $48.2M. Forecast confidence is **moderate** because the range is driven by observable pipeline conversion, churn, and hiring decisions, but late-stage close timing remains volatile.

### Top risks

1. **Pipeline conversion:** Q3 volume misses may persist if enterprise sales cycles remain extended.
2. **GTM efficiency:** Sales and Marketing are over budget while conversion is below plan.
3. **Runway compression:** A downside conversion case combined with approved hiring would reduce strategic flexibility.

### Recommended actions

1. Hold incremental Sales and Marketing hiring until conversion and sales-cycle duration improve for two consecutive months.
2. Reallocate up to $300K of H2 event and demand-generation spend toward partner and expansion programs with measurable pipeline attribution.
3. Run a monthly downside trigger review using churn, stage conversion, DSO, and ending cash; switch to the 0.82x hiring plan if two triggers miss.

## So what?

Northstar should continue investing in growth, but capital release must be tied to conversion evidence so the company protects at least twelve months of runway.

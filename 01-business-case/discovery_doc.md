# Northstar SaaS Discovery Document

## Company context

Northstar is a fictional, three-year-old B2B SaaS company entering FY2026 at approximately $40M ARR. It sells SMB, Mid-Market, Enterprise, and Strategic subscriptions through inbound, outbound, partner, and event channels. FP&A supports the Q3 board process with an integrated revenue, expense, cash, and KPI outlook.

## Board questions

- Are revenue and ARR tracking to plan?
- What operational drivers explain each material budget variance?
- What is the realistic FY2026 outlook under Base, Upside, and Downside cases?
- Does the company have enough cash runway to preserve strategic flexibility?

## Stakeholder goals

- **CFO:** explain the Q3 miss without losing sight of cost discipline, forecast confidence, and runway.
- **CEO:** preserve a credible growth narrative by separating timing pressure from structural demand weakness.
- **Board:** see quantified choices, downside protection, and explicit leading indicators for the next decision gate.
- **Functional leaders:** understand spend accountability and which hiring decisions remain funded.

## Decision frame

The recommended Base case assumes 1.1% monthly logo churn, 0.65% monthly expansion, a 55% realization factor on stage-weighted pipeline, and hiring at the approved pace. Upside and Downside cases alter churn, pipeline conversion, sales-cycle timing, gross margin, and hiring pace independently rather than applying a blanket revenue haircut.

## Deliverables

The primary deliverable is a formula-driven Excel model with a linked 3-statement forecast. Supporting outputs are an eight-slide board deck, variance commentary, a one-page CFO memo, documented assumptions, and reproducible source-data generators.

## So what?

The project is designed around the decisions a real CFO and board make: protect cash, diagnose execution gaps, and fund growth only where conversion evidence supports it.

# FY2026 Budget Variance Commentary

## Revenue

- **January: $51K unfavorable.** Lower new-logo volume more than offset favorable price/mix. This is modest but crossed the $50K board threshold.
- **February: $96K unfavorable.** Continued conversion softness and deal slippage drove the miss; pricing remained resilient.
- **March: $68K favorable.** Quarter-end enterprise closes and richer mix recovered the early-quarter gap.
- **April: $89K favorable.** Higher close volume and improved enterprise mix produced the strongest first-half beat.
- **June: $146K unfavorable.** Several late-stage deals slipped beyond quarter end, creating a timing-driven miss.
- **July: $200K unfavorable.** The Q3 shortfall was concentrated in fewer closed logos, partially offset by stronger ARPA.
- **August: $158K unfavorable.** Slower pipeline conversion persisted; price/mix reduced but did not eliminate the volume gap.
- **September: $78K unfavorable.** Quarter-end execution improved, but not enough to recover July-August slippage. Q3 revenue finished **$437K below budget**.
- **November: $71K favorable; December: $151K favorable.** Improved conversion and year-end enterprise mix support a partial recovery, but the Base case does not assume all Q3 slippage closes.

## Operating expense

- **Marketing: $550K unfavorable / 8.4%.** Incremental demand-generation programs and event commitments exceeded plan. This is the largest controllable FY variance.
- **Sales: $454K unfavorable / 3.9%.** Hiring and commission expense ran ahead of budget despite weaker Q3 conversion, creating an efficiency mismatch.
- **Product & Engineering: $269K unfavorable / 1.8%.** Targeted technical hiring and cloud tooling drove a manageable overrun.
- **Customer Success: $136K favorable / 2.8%.** Hiring timing and lower onboarding contractor use offset part of the GTM overspend.
- **G&A: $267K favorable / 4.4%.** Delayed backfills and professional-fee discipline provided the largest offset.
- **Total FY opex: $871K unfavorable.** Marketing and Sales overruns more than offset Customer Success and G&A savings.

## Management interpretation

The revenue miss is primarily a conversion and timing issue, but spending has not yet adjusted to the weaker Q3 evidence. Management should gate incremental Sales and Marketing hiring until pipeline conversion and sales-cycle duration recover for two consecutive months.

## So what?

Northstar can preserve the growth thesis, but the board should require proof of conversion improvement before funding additional GTM capacity; otherwise the downside case should become the operating plan.
